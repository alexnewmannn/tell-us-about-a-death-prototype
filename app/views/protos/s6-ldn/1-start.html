When someone dies, you can let most government organisations know in one go.

As soon as you can, you'll need to give some details about the person who died. This information will be passed on to the following organisations:

  • HM Revenue and Customs – to deal with any taxes and benefits
  • Department for Work and Pensions – to deal with any benefits and State Pension
  • Passport Office – to cancel a British passport
  • Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency – to cancel a driving licence
  • the local council – to deal with Council Tax, any Blue Badge, any Housing Benefit, inform council housing services and remove the person from the electoral register

Before you start

You'll need:

  • the name of the person who died
  • their National Insurance number
  • their date of death
  • their date of birth
  • their most recent postcode
  • your National Insurance number
Start now

Staying updated

After you have given details about the death, you can choose to get updates on what's happening.

You’ll need to prove your identity before you can get updates. There’s information about how to do this at the end of this service.
